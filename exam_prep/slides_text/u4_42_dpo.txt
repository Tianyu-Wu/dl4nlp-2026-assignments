Recap of RLHF (Ouyang et al., 2022)

We want to train a LM to maximize a reward r(x, y) that models
high-level text quality

In RLHF, the reward is a learned model
trained on human preferences

The reward model is trained using the Bradley–Terry binary
preference model

P (y1 ≻ y0) = σ(rθ(x, y1) − rθ(x, y0))

Direct Preference Optimization: key points

Trains on preferences without reinforcement learning

Simpler to implement: reward model not mandatory, no value
network, no sampling/rollouts

Competitive with PPO in practice

DPO: takeaways

Trains on preference data directly, no RL

Competitive with RL-based training approaches

Used in many LLMs (as of 2025): Qwen, Phi, OLMo, Mixtral…

The pipeline is often SFT→DPO→RL
